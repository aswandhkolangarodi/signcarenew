Course Curriculum

01
Branches of Psychology

Explore the diverse fields within psychology and understand how different branches contribute to our understanding of human behavior and mental processes.

  • Overview of major psychology branches
  • Historical development of psychological fields
  • Interconnections between different branches
  • Career paths in various psychology fields
02
Educational Psychology

Study how people learn and develop in educational settings, and apply psychological principles to improve teaching and learning outcomes.

  • Learning theories and cognitive development
  • Instructional design and pedagogical methods
  • Assessment and evaluation techniques
  • Special education and learning disabilities
  • Classroom management and motivation
03
Developmental Psychology

Understand human development across the lifespan, from infancy through old age, including physical, cognitive, and socioemotional changes.

  • Prenatal and infant development
  • Childhood and adolescent development
  • Adult development and aging
  • Theories of human development (Piaget, Erikson, Vygotsky)
  • Developmental milestones and assessment
04
Personality Psychology

Examine individual differences in behavior, thought patterns, and emotional responses that define personality and influence human interactions.

  • Major personality theories (Freud, Jung, Rogers)
  • Trait theories and the Big Five model
  • Personality assessment tools (MBTI, NEO-PI-R)
  • Personality disorders and abnormal psychology
  • Cultural influences on personality development
05
Behavioral Psychology

Master the principles of behaviorism and learn how environmental factors shape and modify behavior through conditioning and reinforcement.

  • Classical conditioning (Pavlov)
  • Operant conditioning (Skinner)
  • Behavior modification techniques
  • Applied behavior analysis (ABA)
  • Behavioral therapy interventions
06
Social Psychology

Investigate how social interactions, relationships, and group dynamics influence individual behavior, attitudes, and beliefs.

  • Social influence and conformity
  • Group dynamics and team behavior
  • Prejudice, stereotypes, and discrimination
  • Interpersonal attraction and relationships
  • Prosocial behavior and aggression
07
Industrial/Organizational Psychology

Apply psychological principles to workplace settings, focusing on employee behavior, organizational culture, and workplace productivity.

  • Employee selection and recruitment
  • Training and development programs
  • Performance appraisal and feedback
  • Leadership and organizational behavior
  • Work motivation and job satisfaction
  • Workplace stress and employee wellness
08
Child Psychology

Specialize in understanding children's mental, emotional, and behavioral development, and learn therapeutic techniques for working with young populations.

  • Child development stages and milestones
  • Common childhood behavioral issues
  • Play therapy techniques
  • Parent-child relationships and attachment
  • School-related problems and interventions
  • Child trauma and resilience
